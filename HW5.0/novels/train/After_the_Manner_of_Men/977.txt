“you have a most excellent quality of nerve, mr. hartridge,” was carfax’s tribute to the audacity. “as you suggest, our field is rather limited. you are perfectly well aware of the fact that highmount and its hospitality stand as the only barrier between us and social starvation. let us try to discover a _modus vivendi_. the verdict is: ‘guilty, with a recommendation to mercy.’ we are willing to give any man’s sense of humor a chance to redeem itself. you quoted scripture at me a moment ago, let me return the compliment: ‘go in peace, and sin no more.’”
