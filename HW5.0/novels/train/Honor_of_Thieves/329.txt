 for reasons, the _port edes_ took the “north about” course; that is, she headed across south of the banks of newfoundland nearly to cape hatteras, and then braved the three-knot current of the gulf stream by passing down the florida channel on the western side of the bahamas. they had carried good weather with them—light head breezes or calms—all the way; and, although coals were dear and the day’s outlay was limited to twenty-eight tons by order, the steamer usually averaged ten and a half knots, despite the unskilfulness of the engine-room staff.
